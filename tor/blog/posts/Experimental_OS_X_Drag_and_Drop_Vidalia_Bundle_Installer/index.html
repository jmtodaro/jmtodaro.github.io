The Tor Blog/ posts/ Experimental OS X Drag and Drop Vidalia Bundle Installer

I asked for community feedback in this post about drag and drop installation of the Vidalia bundle for Apple's OS X. In working with the Vidalia team, we now have a drag and drop installer. This is experimental. It's designed for a clean install. It won't migrate your settings, nor will it configure anything for you. Upon installing, your milk may sour and your salt may run off with your pepper. Now that the disclaimers are over, here's what it contains and does do for you. read more »

It includes Universal binaries for:

  • Vidalia version 0.2.0-svn r3425
  • Polipo 1.0.4 configured to use Tor as a socksproxy
  • Tor 0.2.1.10-alpha compiled with prefix and bindir set to /Applications/Vidalia.app

Depends where your polipo is listening.

I use polipo with its default port of 8123, so in my Torbutton preferences I
selected "Use custom proxy settings" and put in localhost 8123 for http and
https proxies, and left localhost 9050 for socks.

You're right that when we switch the packages from including privoxy to
including polipo, we'll want to make sure that the Torbutton that's included
knows what port it should tell Firefox to proxy to.

The easy answer is to configure the polipo we ship so that it listens on port
8118. The only downside is that it will conflict if you have Privoxy installed too.

From the post itself, this is experimental. I warned you it might not work, might crash, might sour your milk. It assumed a clean install. if you have a ~/.vidalia or ~/Library/Vidalia directory, it won't work after drag and drop. you will have to change the paths.

As for polipo, diskcache is disabled, otherwise it records every site you've visited through polipo. This is not good for anonymity.

Memory cache is enabled to improve performance.

Comment 20

My bundle didn't work out of the box, until I discovered that polipo was running its default configuration, rather than the one provided by the Vidalia bundle.

The solution was to open Vidalia's preferences, and under "Proxy Application Arguments" in the General tab to add the following:

-c /Applications/Vidalia.app/Contents/Resources/polipo.conf

Then everything worked perfectly.
